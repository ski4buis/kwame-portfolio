About
Comfort isn't stability.

I used to think comfort meant stability. I learned the opposite.

The shift happened in Richmond, in a room with Matthew Pezzimenti breaking down how passive income works at scale. I walked in assuming the path was good grades, good job, trade hours for dollars, retire. I walked out understanding that time-for-money has a ceiling, and ownership, systems, and leverage are how you break through it.

I'm a freshman Mechanical Engineering student at Old Dominion. The degree is a tool, not the destination. I'm building the technical depth, the network, and the discipline to compound over the next 40 years.

Operating philosophy
  • 01
    Leverage over labor
    Time-for-money has a ceiling. Systems, ownership, and compounding break through it.
  • 02
    Systems over randomness
    Repeatable process beats one-time luck. If it can't be rebuilt, it doesn't count.
  • 03
    Discipline over motivation
    Motivation is a feeling. Discipline is a decision already made. This one costs me social time, and I pay it.
  • 04
    Ownership over employment
    Equity compounds. Salaries plateau. I'd rather build something than rent my hours.
  • 05
    Progress over comfort
    Complacency is the thief of progress. Settling is the real risk, not failing.
